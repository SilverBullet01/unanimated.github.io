Commie Editor Guidelines

General Guidelines

Use American English and proper grammar
—"Italicize," "realize," "honor," "color," etc.
—"Toward" and "afterward" rather than "towards" and "afterwards."
—An exception to the American English rule is quotation marks. Just do it right if you are going to use American or British English style for quotation marks.
—An exception to the proper grammar rule would be if you wanted to accurately portray a speech impediment, an accent, a dialect, and so on. In such cases, it may be necessary and possibly even encouraged to use unconventional English.

Use "All right" instead of "alright."

Use "Okay" or "okay" instead of "OK," "Ok," or "ok."

Use contractions as often as possible and as appropriate. Just make sure it reads well before changing.
—Ex.: "I would've done things differently" vs. "I'd have done things differently" vs. "I would have done things differently."

Prefer localization over literal translations. The dialogue should read as smoothly as the show intends.
—Ex.: "It can't be helped" could instead be "I have no choice," "It's no use," or "Shit happens."
—If something reads like shit, change it. Remember, though, that you are NOT a scriptwriter. Don't make changes unless you are sure your line means what it should mean.
—A general rule for Japanese-y words: If you can't find a word in the dictionary (Dictionary.com or Merriam-Webster), it should be localized or translated.

Nuke honorifics and localize them appropriately.
—Generally, this means getting rid of -chan/-kun. -san could be eliminated or changed to Mr. or Mrs. depending on context.
— -sensei should be Mr./Teacher/Professor etc.

Names should be written how they are spoken.
—That usually means surname followed by first name.
—An exception may be a show that very obviously takes place in America, but the above rule should take care of everything.

If someone is speaking and he is not at the scene, italicize his dialogue.

No semicolons.

No double hyphens. Use an em-dash for interrupted dialogue: Alt+0151 gives "—"

Use a spellchecker.

For stuttering, keep the capitalization of the repeated letter or syllable. Examples:
—"D-Don't look at me!"
—"It's so c-cold."



Helpful Hints

Comments in .ass files are enclosed by curly brackets "{" and "}"
—Ex.: {this will not show up on the actual video}

Here are some useful tags:
—"{\an8}" moves a line of text to the top center of the screen. Aegisub script
—"{\i1}" makes text italics until it reaches "{\i0}" or the end of a line. Aegisub script


You can edit scripts in Notepad, an EtherPad, or Aegisub.
