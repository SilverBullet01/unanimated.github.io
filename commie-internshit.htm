Read, do this timing test, READ AGAIN, fix your timing test.
Pay attention to all the info and values for lead in/out, line linking, and keyframe snapping.

Things to pay extra attention to [aka "where you'll usually fuck up"]:

• Don't have bleeds!
• Don't have lead ins over 400 ms!
• Don't have gaps between 2 lines under ~500 ms! [unless the first ends on a keyframe]
• Check your work before handing it over! ["I thought I fixed that" is not an excuse]

Most of the questions you may have are already answered in those guides.

Things to pay extra attention to [aka "where you'll usually fuck up"]:

• Don't use large [filesize] and/or crappy fonts.
• Pay attention to borders/shadows and exact colors and try to match them.
• Use blur and do it right!

Notes:
Typesetting an episode may take anywhere between 2 minutes and a few days.
[...but commonly under an hour, and usually not more than 2-3 hours]
Learn the basics for static signs properly first,
before you start worrying about moving & animated signs.

Check the "Scripts" section in the guide and learn to use scripts.
They will make things faster and easier.
